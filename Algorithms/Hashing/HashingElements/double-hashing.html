Double Hashing: In-Depth Explanation

What is Double Hashing?

Double hashing is a collision resolution technique in hash tables that uses a second hash function to determine the step size for probing. This method helps to reduce clustering and improves the distribution of entries in the hash table.

Key Characteristics:

  • Probe Sequence: When a collision occurs, the next position to check is determined by the second hash function.
  • Formula: h'(k) = (h1(k) + i * h2(k)) mod m
    • where h1(k) is the primary hash function, h2(k) is the secondary hash function, and i is the probe number (1, 2, 3, ...).
    • m is the table size.
